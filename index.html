How it all started

1976 Larry Mullens posted a notice in his school saying 'musicians wanted' to form a band. So in Larry's kitchen on the 26th of September 1976 seven musicans took part in what would be the bands first practice and the name of band was 'Feedback'. By 1978 the band which had gone through two name changes to 'Hype' and then finally U2 was down to the four that we know today Bono on vocals, the Edge on guitar, Adam Clayton on base and Larry Mullins Jr on drums. Despite building a solid reputation locally U2 struggled to get a record deal until in 1979 U2 played a show at the 2,000-seat National Stadium in Dublin as part of an Irish tour. Although they took a significant risk in booking a venue of that size, the move paid off. Bill Stewart, an A&R representative for Island Records, was in attendance and subsequently signed the group to the label and the rest as they say was history!

Band Members

Bono

Whose real name is Paul David Hewson, born 10 May 1960, is an Irish singer-songwriter, venture capitalist, businessman, philanthropist and lead vocalist, better known as Bono. He soon established himself as a passionate frontman for the band through his expressive vocal style and grandiose gestures and songwriting. His lyrics are known for their social and political themes, and for their religious imagery inspired by his Christian beliefs.

The Edge

Also known as David Howell Evans, born 8 August 1961, The Edge is a British-born Irish musician and songwriter best known as the lead guitarist, keyboardist, and backing vocalist of the rock band U2. A member of the group since its inception, he has recorded 14 studio albums with the band and one solo record

Larry Mullins Jr

Born 31 October 1961, Larry Mullen Jr is an Irish musician and actor, best known as the drummer and co-founder of the rock band U2. Mullen's distinctive, almost military drumming style developed from his playing martial beats in a childhood marching band, the Artane Boys Band.

Adam Clayton

Born the 13 March 1960, Adam Clayton is an English-born Irish musician and the bass guitarist of the rock band U2. Clayton attended Mount Temple Comprehensive School, where he met schoolmates with whom he co-founded U2 in 1976. Clayton's bass guitar playing style is noted for its "harmonic syncopation", giving the music a driving rhythm

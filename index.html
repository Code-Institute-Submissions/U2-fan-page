How it all started

In 1976 Larry Mullens posted a notice in his school saying 'musicians wanted' to form a band. So in Larry's kitchen on the 26th of September 1976 seven musicans took part in what would be the bands first practice and the name of band was 'Feedback'. By 1978 the band which had gone through two name changes to 'Hype' and then finally U2 was down to the four that we know today Bono on vocals, the Edge on guitar, Adam Clayton on base and Larry Mullins Jr on drums. Despite building a solid reputation locally U2 struggled to get a record deal until finally in 1979 they signed with Island records and the rest as they say was history!

Band Members

Bono

whose real name is Paul David Hewson, born 10 May 1960, is an Irish singer-songwriter, venture capitalist, businessman, and philanthropist. Bono soon established himself as a passionate frontman for the band through his expressive vocal style and grandiose gestures and songwriting. His lyrics are known for their social and political themes, and for their religious imagery inspired by his Christian beliefs

The Edge

also know as David Howell Evans born 8 August 1961, is a British-born Irish musician and songwriter best known as the lead guitarist, keyboardist, and backing vocalist of the rock band U2. A member of the group since its inception, he has recorded 14 studio albums with the band and one solo record

Larry Mullins Jr

born 31 October 1961 is an Irish musician and actor, best known as the drummer and co-founder of the rock band U2. Mullen's distinctive, almost military drumming style developed from his playing martial beats in a childhood marching band, the Artane Boys Band.

Adam Clayton

Clayton's bass guitar playing style is noted for its "harmonic syncopation", giving the music a driving rhythm
